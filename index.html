Agreement Overview

This Memorandum of Understanding (the "MOU") is entered into as of May 27, 2026 (the "Effective Date"), by and between Neighborhood Kids Schools ("NKS") and AUDIA Consulting ("AUDIA Consulting"). NKS and AUDIA Consulting may each be referred to as a "Party" and together as the "Parties."

Party One Neighborhood Kids Schools
Party Two AUDIA Consulting
Effective Date May 27, 2026
1. Purpose and Shared Mission

1.1 Purpose

NKS and AUDIA Consulting desire to collaborate in expanding access to Christ-centered, high-quality early childhood education through missionally aligned church partnerships.

AUDIA Consulting has regional and national relationships with churches, church networks, pastors, ministry leaders, and related organizations. NKS has an operating model for launching and managing faith-based preschool programs in partnership with local churches. This MOU sets forth the Parties' mutual understanding regarding referrals, responsibilities, compensation, confidentiality, and related terms.

1.2 Shared Mission

NKS exists to empower families and churches to achieve their God-given potential through exceptional Christ-centered early childhood education in partnership with churches across the United States.

AUDIA Consulting works with leaders and organizations pursuing work of significance, helping them steward relationships, opportunities, and influence toward meaningful outcomes.

The Parties share a desire to help churches, children, families, and communities flourish through faithful, practical, and sustainable ministry partnerships.

2. Definitions

2.1 Qualified Referred Church

"Qualified Referred Church" means a church, church network, church-affiliated ministry, existing church preschool, or church-controlled education opportunity that:

  1. Is first introduced to NKS by AUDIA Consulting, or is materially advanced by AUDIA Consulting through relationship mapping, endorsement, facilitation, or direct involvement;
  2. Is not already in NKS's active written pipeline before AUDIA Consulting's referral, unless AUDIA Consulting materially advances the opportunity beyond NKS's existing relationship;
  3. Enters into a Church Agreement with NKS or an NKS affiliate during the term of this MOU or within the referral protection period described in Section 6; and
  4. Is not expressly declined in writing by NKS before AUDIA Consulting makes a material introduction or facilitation effort.

For clarity, a church network, denominational leader, multisite church, ministry leader, or other relationship introduced or materially advanced by AUDIA Consulting may qualify if that relationship results in one or more churches entering into Church Agreements with NKS.

2.2 Church Agreement

"Church Agreement" means any signed ministry affiliation, lease, operating, management, acquisition, merger, preschool takeover, services, or similar agreement that authorizes NKS or an NKS affiliate to operate, manage, own, or launch a preschool or early childhood education program at or through a Qualified Referred Church.

2.3 Opening Date and Active Operations

"Opening Date" means the first date on which NKS or an NKS affiliate begins licensed preschool operations at or through a Qualified Referred Church.

"Active Operations" means that NKS or an NKS affiliate remains in active agreement with the Qualified Referred Church and is operating a licensed preschool or early childhood education program at or through that church.

3. Engagement Process

3.1 Church List and Pre-Contact Vetting

AUDIA Consulting and NKS will collaborate to identify a strategic list of potential church partners from AUDIA Consulting's network whose values, location, facilities, demographics, leadership, and mission alignment appear to fit the NKS model.

When appropriate, AUDIA Consulting may share potential church opportunities with NKS and Jenn Rook, NKS's Director of Development and Church Partnerships, before outreach so NKS can help assess feasibility, market fit, and priority.

3.2 Outreach, Introduction, and Follow-Up

Brian Audia or another AUDIA Consulting representative may initiate contact with a church's Lead Pastor, Executive Pastor, or other senior leader by text, phone, email, in-person conversation, or other appropriate relationship channel to gauge interest in a conversation with NKS.

If the church leader responds positively, AUDIA Consulting will make a warm introduction connecting the church leader with Frank Toral, CEO of NKS, and, when appropriate, Jenn Rook or another NKS representative.

After the warm introduction, NKS will be primarily responsible for coordinating follow-up conversations, feasibility analysis, site assessment, agreement negotiation, licensing, operational planning, staffing, curriculum training, and launch execution.

3.3 Ongoing Relationship Support

AUDIA Consulting may continue to support the relationship where its involvement adds value, including follow-up encouragement, pastoral context, relationship translation, or assistance overcoming initial objections. AUDIA Consulting is not responsible for running formal discovery, negotiating church agreements, or quarterbacking the final close unless the Parties mutually agree otherwise for a specific opportunity.

4. Compensation

4.1 Payment Structure

Year 1 $10,000 success fee due within 10 business days after Church Agreement execution.
Years 2 Through 10 Nine additional $10,000 annual payments, if Active Operations continue.
Maximum Per Church $100,000 total over 10 years, subject to continued Active Operations.

For each Qualified Referred Church that enters into a Church Agreement with NKS or an NKS affiliate, NKS shall pay AUDIA Consulting a Year 1 success fee of $10,000. This fee shall be due within ten (10) business days after execution of the applicable Church Agreement.

The Parties acknowledge that this Year 1 success fee is the first of the ten (10) intended annual payments for that Qualified Referred Church.

For each Qualified Referred Church, NKS shall pay AUDIA Consulting nine (9) additional annual payments of $10,000 each, covering Years 2 through 10, so long as Active Operations continue.

The Year 2 payment shall be due within ten (10) business days after the first anniversary of the Opening Date. Each subsequent annual payment shall be due within ten (10) business days after each anniversary of the Opening Date thereafter, through the Year 10 payment.

4.2 Payment Cessation, Affiliates, and Reporting

If a Qualified Referred Church exits its agreement with NKS, or if NKS or its affiliate ceases Active Operations at or through that church, future annual payments attributable to that church shall cease as of the date Active Operations end. Payments already made to AUDIA Consulting are fully earned, non-refundable, and not subject to clawback.

If a Qualified Referred Church enters into a Church Agreement with an NKS affiliate, successor, assignee, related entity, or controlled operating entity, the compensation obligations in this Section 4 shall apply as if the Church Agreement had been entered into directly by NKS. NKS shall remain responsible for ensuring payment to AUDIA Consulting unless AUDIA Consulting expressly agrees in writing to release NKS from that obligation.

At least once per calendar quarter, NKS shall provide AUDIA Consulting a written status update identifying each Qualified Referred Church, current pipeline status, Church Agreement date if applicable, projected or actual Opening Date, Active Operations status, payments due, and payments made under this MOU.

5. No Exclusivity or Minimum Volume

This MOU does not create exclusive territories, exclusive church lists, or minimum referral obligations unless separately agreed in writing. AUDIA Consulting is not required to produce any minimum number of referrals, and NKS is not required to accept, pursue, or close any specific referral.

6. Referral Protection After Termination

Termination or non-renewal of this MOU shall not affect NKS's obligation to pay compensation for any Qualified Referred Church introduced, identified, or materially advanced by AUDIA Consulting before the effective date of termination or non-renewal, provided that the church enters into a Church Agreement with NKS or an NKS affiliate within twenty-four (24) months after the effective date of termination or non-renewal.

For any Qualified Referred Church that enters into a Church Agreement before termination or during the twenty-four (24) month referral protection period, all payment obligations under Section 4 shall survive termination or non-renewal for so long as Active Operations continue, subject to the Year 10 cap.

7. Confidentiality and Limited Non-Solicitation

7.1 Mutual Confidentiality

Each Party may receive non-public business, financial, operational, relationship, ministry, strategic, or other confidential information from the other Party. This includes referral economics, target lists, relationship information, church contacts, strategic plans, operating information, financial terms, and non-public information regarding church opportunities.

Each Party shall keep the other Party's confidential information confidential and shall use it only for purposes of this MOU. Each Party may disclose confidential information to its legal, tax, financial, insurance, or business advisors on a need-to-know basis, provided those advisors are informed of the confidential nature of the information. A Party may also disclose confidential information if required by law, subpoena, court order, or other legal process, provided the disclosing Party gives the other Party prompt notice where legally permitted.

Confidential information does not include information that is publicly available, independently developed without use of the other Party's confidential information, lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality restriction, or already known by the receiving Party before disclosure.

7.2 Limited Non-Solicitation

During the term of this MOU and for twelve (12) months thereafter, AUDIA Consulting shall not knowingly solicit a Qualified Referred Church that is in Active Operations with NKS to terminate or materially reduce its relationship with NKS, or to replace NKS with a competing early childhood education provider.

For opportunities that have not yet entered into a Church Agreement, AUDIA Consulting shall not knowingly use NKS confidential information to steer an active NKS opportunity to a competing early childhood education provider while NKS is actively pursuing that opportunity.

This Section 7.2 does not prohibit AUDIA Consulting from:

  1. Maintaining preexisting relationships with churches, pastors, ministry leaders, denominations, networks, or other organizations;
  2. Serving churches in capacities unrelated to replacing NKS as an early childhood education provider;
  3. Working with early education partners for churches that are not Qualified Referred Churches;
  4. Working with early education partners for churches NKS has declined in writing; or
  5. Working with early education partners for churches where NKS has not had substantive activity for at least one hundred eighty (180) days, provided AUDIA Consulting does not use NKS confidential information to do so.
8. Legal and Administrative Terms

8.1 Independent Contractors; No Authority to Bind

The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this MOU creates a partnership, joint venture, franchise, employment relationship, fiduciary relationship, agency relationship, or legal representative relationship between the Parties.

Neither Party may bind the other Party, make commitments on behalf of the other Party, or represent that it has authority to do so unless the other Party gives express written authorization.

8.2 Term, Renewal, and Termination

This MOU shall begin on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of one (1) year. After the initial term, this MOU shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year terms unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the expiration of the then-current term.

Either Party may terminate this MOU for any reason upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party.

Sections 4, 6, 7, 8.3, 8.4, 8.5, and any other provision that by its nature should survive shall survive termination or non-renewal.

8.3 Notices

Any formal notice under this MOU shall be in writing and delivered by personal delivery, nationally recognized overnight courier, certified mail, or email with confirmation of receipt to the addresses or email addresses designated by the Parties in writing.

8.4 Governing Law and Binding Arbitration

This MOU shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to conflict-of-law rules.

Before either Party initiates arbitration, the Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve the dispute through executive-level discussion between Brian Audia and Frank Toral, or their designated successors. If the dispute is not resolved within thirty (30) days after written notice of the dispute, either Party may proceed to binding arbitration as set forth below.

Any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to this MOU, including its formation, interpretation, performance, breach, termination, enforceability, or payment obligations, shall be resolved by final and binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted by one neutral arbitrator. The seat and venue of arbitration shall be Collin County, Texas, unless the Parties mutually agree in writing to another location or to remote proceedings.

The arbitrator may award all remedies available at law or in equity, including damages, specific performance, injunctive relief, declaratory relief, interest, costs, and reasonable attorneys' fees where authorized by this MOU or applicable law. The arbitrator's award shall be final and binding, and judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

Either Party may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to protect confidential information, prevent misuse of relationship information, preserve the status quo, or prevent irreparable harm, without waiving the obligation to arbitrate the underlying dispute. For any court proceeding permitted under this Section 8.4, the Parties consent to exclusive venue in the state or federal courts located in Collin County, Texas.

8.5 Assignment, Amendments, and Electronic Signatures

Neither Party may assign this MOU without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that NKS may assign this MOU to an affiliate, successor, or entity acquiring substantially all of NKS's relevant assets or operations, provided that the assignee assumes NKS's obligations under this MOU in writing. No assignment shall relieve NKS of payment obligations accrued before the assignment unless AUDIA Consulting agrees in writing.

This MOU represents the Parties' entire understanding regarding the subject matter described herein and supersedes prior oral or written discussions regarding that subject matter. Any amendment or modification must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

This MOU may be signed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one agreement. Electronic signatures and scanned signature copies shall be treated as originals for all purposes.

9. Good-Faith Collaboration

The Parties enter this MOU in a spirit of good-faith collaboration and shared mission. The Parties agree to communicate regularly, share practical learning from church conversations, and work together toward sustainable partnerships that serve churches, families, children, and communities well.
